Taiwan Receipt
Lottery Checker in Python

Winning lottery numbers obtained from the Taiwan lottery website

Known in Chinese as 統一發票 (Tǒngyī Fāpiào), the bi-monthly receipt invoice lottery in Taiwan was created to encourage legal tax reporting by giving consumers an incentive to purchase at stores that legally report sales taxes. Every two months, lottery numbers are randomly drawn that correspond to the eight numbers printed along the top of vendors' receipts and posted on a Taiwanese government website.

As the chances of winning the lottery for the smallest amount are relatively high (approximately 1/200 receipts), many people in Taiwan will store their receipts and check them when the new lottery numbers are posted. However, this process involves checking by eye each 8-digit receipt number against the several winning numbers posted online which, depending on the number of receipts, can be tedious and time consuming.

Therefore, I wrote a Python program that can check a Google Sheets file full of lottery numbers input by a user (via scanning the QR code located on the receipt) against the winning lottery numbers posted online by the Taiwanese government every two months. The user then receives an email with any winning numbers. This program saves hours of time and effort in playing this lottery. I tested my app over a two month period with my own saved receipts. As a result I personally won the lottery twice for the smallest amount (~$10).

If I were to extend the project further I would create a mobile app that could automatically check the lottery after the QR code for the receipt was scanned.
